The Neuroscience

The brain doesn't remember the past. It constructs the future.

Daniel Schacter and Donna Rose Addis discovered that the hippocampal system that stores memories is the same system that imagines futures. Memory is not playback. It is a construction kit. The brain disassembles past experiences into elements and recombines them into novel scenarios.

Patients with hippocampal damage cannot imagine novel scenes, not just remember old ones. Imagination and memory are the same machinery, running in different directions.

The Specification

What if you could make any AI build like an Imagineer?

Imagineer Mode is a process specification. Not a persona. Not a prompt. A document that restructures how an intelligence constructs. From plan-then-execute to build-to-discover. From abstract to sensory. From analytical to experiential.

Seven principles extracted from constructive imagination neuroscience and Walt Disney Imagineering. Each one changes how things get built. The spec IS the implementation. Give it to any AI and the construction process changes.

Output Layers

Six layers that make the process visible

Click each layer to explore
Experience Walkthroughs always present
First-person, sensory, temporal journeys through the imagined experience. You walk through the door. The light shifts. You feel like you are there.
Blue Sky Cards numbered cards
Unconstrained "what if" possibilities. Numbered. The rule: dream in one breath, evaluate in the next. Never in the same.
Sensory Annotations sensory:
Texture, sound, light, spatial quality notes embedded inline. Must be specific and evocative. Warm oak, brass worn smooth. Not "nice materials."
Prototype Sketches rough is the point
Rough constructions generated as thinking tools. ASCII layouts, scenario drafts, minimal viable concepts. Deliberately unpolished. Polish kills learning.
Plus Notes +
"What would make this extraordinary instead of good?" Genuinely transformative, not incremental. The last 10% that carries 90% of the impact.
Story Threads thread traces
Narrative connections through everything built. Does every element serve the central story? If something cannot be traced back, it is flagged.

Experiences before it describes

02

Dreams before it constrains

Blue sky first. Engineering second. The sequence is sacred. The best ideas die when evaluation happens during generation.

Structures through story

Narrative is not decoration. It is the gravitational center that organises every detail. When the story is strong, decisions become easy.

04

Builds to discover what thinking cannot find

Rough prototypes appear as the primary mode of reasoning. The artifact talks back. The sketch reveals what the plan never contained.

05

Plus it until it is undeniable

Every version can be elevated. The last 10% that carries 90% of the impact. Generative delight, not perfectionist anxiety.

Origin

Built from two unlikely sources that turned out to be the same source. The neuroscience of how the brain constructs future experience - Schacter's constructive episodic simulation, Schon's reflection-in-action, Tversky's mind in motion. And 70 years of Walt Disney Imagineering - the most successful experience design methodology in human history.

The Imagineering principles were not arbitrary. They mapped precisely onto how the brain actually constructs. Start from the experience. Dream before constraining. Let narrative be the structure. Make it sensory. Build to learn. Plus everything. Design the journey.

Seven principles. Six output layers. A continuous dial from 0 to 100. And a new axis in the architecture: Process. How you build.
