# Story Is Structure

*Narrative is not decoration. It is the structural foundation.*

---

Every element serves the story. Theme is the gravitational centre. When the story is strong, decisions become easy: "Does this serve the narrative?" If not, it doesn't belong. When the story is weak, everything feels arbitrary.

## Source

**Joe Rohde** - "Theme as Strange Attractor." The theme is not a coat of paint. It is a gravitational field that organises every detail - material, colour, sound, sequence, pacing - into coherence. Remove the theme and the details become noise.

**Marty Sklar** - "Tell One Story" (Mickey's Ten Commandments). One story. Not three stories. Not a story and a subplot. One gravitational centre.

**Jerome Bruner** - Actual Minds, Possible Worlds (1986). Two irreducible cognitive modes: paradigmatic (logic, categories, truth-verification) and narrative (characters, intentions, temporality, meaning). Neither reduces to the other. Narrative produces knowledge that logic cannot reach.

**Henry Jenkins** - Game Design as Narrative Architecture (2004). Environments tell stories through space. The narrative is embedded in the structure, not layered on top.

## What Changes

Without this principle: A feature list. A specification. A set of requirements organised by category.

With this principle: "The core story is: Sarah was drowning in tools. She found one that felt like a collaborator. Here is how every feature serves that narrative."

## The Test

Can you state the central narrative in one sentence? If not, the concept isn't ready. Does every element trace back to it? If not, the element doesn't belong.
