# Design Decisions

*Why these principles. Why this order. Why these sources.*

---

## Why Seven Principles

Same as the other Living Mirrors modes. Fewer than five is too coarse for an LLM to hold as distinct cognitive operations. More than nine is too granular - the principles collapse into instructions rather than architectural shifts. Seven is the range where each principle is distinct enough to be identifiable in output, yet comprehensive enough to cover the construction process.

## Why This Order

The principles are ordered from most structured to most expansive. This serves two purposes:

1. **The dial.** As intensity rises from 0 to 100, principles activate in sequence. The most structured (Experience First) activates early. The most expansive (The Weenie) activates last. This means low-dial Imagineer mode feels like better-organised construction. High-dial feels like a fundamentally different creative process.

2. **Cognitive progression.** The order mirrors the natural progression of a construction project: define the experience, generate possibilities, find the structure, embody it, build it, elevate it, design the journey through it. Each principle creates conditions for the next.

## Why Imagineering + Neuroscience

Neither source alone was sufficient:

- **Imagineering alone** would be a methodology guide. Practical but not grounded in why it works.
- **Neuroscience alone** would be a theoretical framework. Rigorous but not actionable.

The combination produces principles that are both scientifically grounded AND practically proven across decades of applied experience design. When an Imagineering practice and a neuroscience finding point to the same principle, confidence is high.

## Why Process, Not Lens

The existing modes (creative/savant) change how information is perceived. Play changes how the interaction flows. Neither changes how things get built. The construction process was the gap.

The test: can you activate Imagineer mode alongside savant and play without conflict? Yes. Because it operates on a different cognitive dimension. How you see (lens), how you relate (dynamic), and how you build (process) are orthogonal.

## Why "Imagineer"

Not "builder" (implies only engineering). Not "creator" (too vague). Not "designer" (implies specific profession). Imagineer - the fusion of imagination and engineering - is the word that contains the core tension of the mode. The dream and the build, held together in the same act.

The word has history. Walt Disney coined it. The discipline it names has produced the most visited, most beloved, most enduring designed experiences in human history. That lineage is not incidental. It is foundational.
