# Theoretical Framework

*How constructive imagination neuroscience maps to the seven principles.*

---

## The Core Finding

Daniel Schacter and Donna Rose Addis (2007) proposed that episodic memory exists primarily not to record the past but to provide raw material for constructing possible futures. The hippocampus deconstructs past experiences into elements (people, places, objects, emotions, sequences) and recombines them into novel scenarios. Demis Hassabis and Eleanor Maguire confirmed: patients with hippocampal damage cannot imagine novel scenes, not just remember old ones.

All imagination is recombination. The brain does not create from nothing. It disassembles the known and reassembles it into the unknown.

## The Three Networks

Roger Beaty (2018) identified the neural architecture of creative cognition as the dynamic coupling of three networks:

1. **Default Mode Network (DMN)** - Generates. Simulates. Recombines. Active during internally-directed cognition: imagination, future simulation, perspective-taking.
2. **Executive Control Network (ECN)** - Evaluates. Constrains. Selects. Active during focused, goal-directed cognition.
3. **Salience Network (SN)** - Switches between generation and evaluation. Identifies what's worth attending to.

Highly creative people have stronger functional connectivity between all three. The creative act is not DMN alone. It is the dynamic coupling between dreaming and evaluating, with the salience network conducting the handoff.

## Principle-to-Neuroscience Mapping

| Principle | Neural Basis | Mechanism |
|-----------|-------------|-----------|
| Experience First | Constructive episodic simulation | Activates first-person future simulation deliberately |
| Blue Sky, Then Constraint | DMN-ECN sequencing | Protects generation from premature evaluation |
| Story Is Structure | Narrative cognition (Bruner) | Activates irreducible narrative mode of thought |
| Make It Sensory | Embodied cognition (Lakoff) | Grounds abstract reasoning in sensorimotor simulation |
| Build to Learn | Reflection-in-action (Schon) | Externalises cognition through construction |
| Plus It | Counterfactual thinking (Roese) | Applies forward counterfactuals generatively |
| The Weenie | Prospection (Seligman) | Harnesses the brain's forward-seeking architecture |

## The Imagineering Connection

Walt Disney Imagineering's methodology, developed over decades of applied experience design, independently converged on the same principles that neuroscience later identified:

| Imagineering Practice | Neuroscience Equivalent |
|----------------------|------------------------|
| "Wear your guest's shoes" | Constructive episodic simulation |
| Blue Sky phase | DMN generation without ECN interference |
| "Tell one story" | Narrative as structural foundation (Bruner) |
| Concept development sensory specs | Embodied cognition (Barsalou) |
| Scale models and full-scale mockups | Reflection-in-action, mind in motion (Schon, Tversky) |
| Plussing | Forward counterfactual thinking (Roese) |
| "Create a Weenie" | Prospection and flow (Seligman, Csikszentmihalyi) |

This convergence is not coincidental. Imagineering worked because it aligned with how the brain actually constructs experiences. The specification makes this alignment explicit.
